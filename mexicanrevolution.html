The Mexican Revolution

The main conflict sparking the Mexican Revolution was the dictatorship of Porfirio Diaz, which the citizens wished to remove from office. Profirio used his access to the military to use ruthless tactics to control the citizens as well as the local and rural police. Profirio also looked to reconcile the liberalism with the church. He sought to unite conservative and liberal elites in the country. He even chose not to enforce anticlerical restrictions adopted by the previous government administration.
